5-dice Yahtzee Rules

Objective

The objective of the game is to get as many points as possible by rolling five dice and getting certain combinations.

Gameplay

In each turn, the player can roll the dice up to three times then chooses a scoring category, although he may stop and score after the first or second roll. After each roll, the player can choose the dice that he wants to keep by clicking on them, so that they won't be changed in the next roll. If the player doesn't want to keep a dice any more, click on it again.

There are totally 14 scoring categories, and the player must choose one in each turn. Each category can be chosen only once. If the player can't (or doesn't want to) enter a score, he must enter a zero. The game ends when all the categories are chosen. Scores are totalled, including any bonus points.

Scoring

The score card is divided into an Upper Section and a Lower Section.

Upper Section

Category Combination Score
1 Dice with number one Total of ones
2 Dice with number two Total of twos
3 Dice with number three Total of threes
4 Dice with number four Total of fours
5 Dice with number five Total of fives
6 Dice with number six Total of sixes

Bonus

If the player scores a total of 63 or more in Upper Section, he can get a 50-point bonus. ("63" is based on getting three of each number on average)

Lower Section

Category Combination Score
2 pairs Two pairs of dice of the same numbers Total of all dice
3 of a kind At least three dice of the same number Total of all dice
4 of a kind At least four dice of the same number Total of all dice
Full House Three dice of a number and two of another 25
Small Straight Four sequential dice
(1,2,3,4 or 2,3,4,5 or 3,4,5,6)		 30
Large Straight Five sequential dice
(1,2,3,4,5 or 2,3,4,5,6)		 40
Chance Any combination Total of all dice
Yahtzee All five dice of the same number 50

Joker Rule

If the player gets a combination of all five dice of the same number, and Yahtzee and the corresponding category in Upper Section have already been chosen, then he can score in any open categories in Lower Section, although this doesn't meet the normal requirement of there categories at all.

For example, you roll five Fours, and you've chosen the "4" and "Yahtzee" categories, then you can score 40 points in "Large Straight" (if not chosen yet).
