6-dice Yahtzee Rules

The basic rules are the same as 5-dice Yahtzee except that six dice are used and there are totally 20 scoring categories, and scoring rules are somewhat different.

Scoring

The score card is divided into an Upper Section and a Lower Section.

Upper Section

Category Combination Score
1 Dice with number one Total of ones
2 Dice with number two Total of twos
3 Dice with number three Total of threes
4 Dice with number four Total of fours
5 Dice with number five Total of fives
6 Dice with number six Total of sixes

Bonus

If the player scores a total of 84 or more in Upper Section, he can get a 100-point bonus. ("84" is based on getting four of each number on average)

Lower Section

Category Combination Score
1 pair A pair of dice of the same numbers Total of the largest pair
2 pairs Two pairs of dice of the same numbers Total of the largest two pairs
3 pairs Three pairs of dice of the same numbers Total of the three pairs
3 of a kind Three dice of the same number Total of the three dice
4 of a kind Four dice of the same number Total of the four dice
5 of a kind Five dice of the same number Total of the five dice
Small Straight 1,2,3,4,5 15
Large Straight 2,3,4,5,6 20
Full Straight 1,2,3,4,5,6 21
Hut Three dice of a number and two of another Total of the five dice
House Three dice of a number and three of another Total of all dice
Tower Four dice of a number and two of another Total of all dice
Chance Any combination Total of all dice
Yahtzee All six dice of the same number 100

Joker Rule

If the player gets a combination of all six dice of the same number, and Yahtzee and the corresponding category in Upper Section have already been chosen, then he can score in any open categories in Lower Section, although this doesn't meet the normal requirement of there categories at all. At this time, the scoring rules of all categories are changed to total of all dice, except "Small Straight", "Large Straight" and "Full Straight".

For example, you roll six Sixes, and you've chosen the "6" and "Yahtzee" categories, then you can score 36 points in "1 pair" or 21 points in "Full Straight" (if not chosen yet).
